Privacy Policy

This is the general privacy policy (“Privacy Policy”) for Alen and/or its corporate affiliates (whether existing now or in the future) (hereinafter referred to collectively as “Alen” or “we”). Except where Alen posts a separate privacy policy, this Privacy Policy applies to all information we collect or acquire from and about consumers: (i) through any media, channels and platforms, whether online, offline, via mobile and wireless platforms, or otherwise, and whether existing now or in the future, as well as Alen-branded applications that operate on or in connection with third party social media sites and platforms, such as apps for Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, and Google/YouTube (all the foregoing, hereinafter referred to as the “Alen Interactive Platform” or “Site”); and (ii) via other collection, acquisitions and marketing channels (e.g., direct mail, paper and phone enrollments, cross-sell programs, affiliate marketing, and our Business Partners (as defined in Section 2 below). 

We are committed to respecting your privacy and recognize your desire to protect the Personally Identifiable Information (as defined in Section 1 below) you share with us. This Privacy Policy describes the types of information we may collect from and/or about you (whether via the Alen Interactive Platform or otherwise), the treatment of such information, and the choices you have regarding your information.  

This Privacy Policy constitutes part of the terms and conditions (such as official rules) that govern your participation in any and all events and activities offered or sponsored by us. 

Notice To California Residents – Your California Privacy Rights: 

Effective January 1, 2005, under California Civil Code Section 1798.83 (known as the “Shine the Light” law), if an individual who is a California resident has provided personal information (as such term is defined by the Shine the Light law) to a business in connection with a business relationship that is primarily for personal, family, or household purposes, and if that business has within the immediately preceding calendar year disclosed such an individual’s personal information to a third-party and knows or should have known that such third-party used the information for its own direct marketing purposes, then that business is obligated to disclose in writing to such individual upon request, what personal information was shared and with whom it was shared. A business may comply with this law by: (1) having EITHER a published privacy policy of not sharing a customer’s personal information for third-parties’ direct marketing use unless the customer has first affirmatively opted in to such sharing OR a published privacy policy of not sharing a customer’s personal information for third-parties’ direct marketing use if the customer has opted out to prevent his/her personal information from being shared for direct marketing use; AND (2) notifying the customer of his/her right to opt out and providing a cost-free means for the customer to exercise that right. 

Any request for a disclosure required under this law should be sent to us via email at service@alen.com. Please note that under this law, we are not required to respond to a customer’s request more than once in a calendar year, nor are we required to respond to any request that is not sent to the email address designated above. 

Types of Information We Collect

1.1 Personally Identifiable Information.  

“Personally Identifiable Information” or “PII” means information relating to an identified or identifiable individual.  
We may also collect PII from third party sources, such as: (1) third party sites or platforms, in the event that you link or associate your Alen profile to that third party site or platform or import your contacts from that third party account; (2) friends or family members, if they have provided your information to Alen or through the Alen Interactive Platform; or (3) through the use of cookies and similar technologies (see Section 5 below for more information). 

1.2  Other Information.  

We may collect other information that cannot identify or be linked to an individual (and is therefore, not PII). For example, we may collect traffic data pertaining to the Alen Interactive Platform, such as total page views in a given time period, total impressions per ad, average amount of time that visitors spend on the Alen Interactive Platform (or a part thereof) each time they visit, average amount of time that visitors spend on each page of any one or more parts of the Alen Interactive Platform, and percentage of visitors from a certain geographical area.  

We typically collect this information through the use of cookies and similar technologies.

For GDPR purposes, we use your PII in the context of these transactional, administrative, and customer relation purposes pursuant to any contracts we have with you, any consent we’ve received from you, or our legitimate interests in: (1) improving our products and service; (2) administering and maintaining operations of the Alen Interactive Platform; (3) offering, marketing and advertising products and services; (4) administering and managing transactions and relationships with you; and (5) personalizing user experiences.

For example, if your prior viewing habits on Alen.com suggest that you are affected more by a certain type of environmental pollutant than others, you might see more ads related to products that we consider to be more useful for such pollutants.

For GDPR purposes, we use your PII for email marketing pursuant to your consent (when we are required by law to obtain it) or our legitimate interests, such as: (1) offering, marketing and advertising products and services; and (2) personalizing user experiences.

For GDPR purposes, we use your PII for market research pursuant to our legitimate interests in: (1) improving our products and service; and (2) offering, marketing and advertising products and services.

For GDPR purposes, we aggregate your PII pursuant to our legitimate interests in: (1) improving our products and service; and (2) personalizing user experiences.

Cookies & Similar Technologies

We may collect information about your computer or device by using cookies and similar technologies. A “cookie” is a small file that is stored on the hard drive of your computer. Cookies are created when a user's browser loads a particular website. The website sends information to the browser which then creates a text file. Every time the user goes back to the same website, the browser retrieves and sends this file to the website's server.  

We use both Session cookies and Persistent cookies on the Alen Site. Session cookies are temporary files that are erased when you close your browser. When you restart your browser and go back to the site that created the cookie, the website will not recognize you. Persistent cookies are files that remain on your hard drive until you erase them or they expire. How long a cookie remains on your browser depends on how long the visited website has programmed the cookie to last.  

We also use Advertising cookies. These cookies will collect information about your visit to our website, the content you viewed, the links you followed and information about your browser, device, and your IP address. We might sometimes share some limited aspects of this data with third parties for advertising purposes. We may also share online data collected through cookies with our advertising partners. This means that when you visit another website, you may be shown advertising based on your browsing patterns on our website.
