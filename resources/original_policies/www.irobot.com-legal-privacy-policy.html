Information We Collect from Registered Devices Equipped with Smart Technology.

 

Some of our Robots are equipped with smart technology which allows the Robots to transmit data wirelessly to the Service. Robot data is stored in a deidentified state (separated from identifiable information). 

 

  • Robot and App usage data. When you register your Robot with the iRobot App, we collect information about the Robot, such as a Robot name and device number, and information about the Robot and/or App usage, such as battery life and health.

For example, the Robot collects information about the level of dirt detection, the Wi-Fi signal strength in each location, and information about the robot's movement throughout the environment to create a location "map" of the Robot’s domain and the existence and type of objects (chair, desk, fridge etc.) or obstacles encountered. We will store the name you give your robot, as well as any "room names," "zone names," or other "custom" names you assign to the map created of the robot's domain.

Doing so will allow iRobot to scan your device for smart home apps in order to suggest smart home connections and to prioritize future smart home automation partners. iRobot will not collect this information unless you opt-in, and you may opt-out at any time. App information will be stored in a deidentifed manner.
  • iRobot Beta Program. You may opt-in to the iRobot Beta Program made available within the App, which enables additional experimental Robot and/or App functionality. Certain features require additional data collection in order to support that particular feature. Each feature in the Beta Program requires your opt-in permission to enable that functionality. Any personal information required to support a Beta feature will be disclosed in the Beta Program section within the App at the time that the new Beta feature is released. The information required to support that Beta feature will not be collected or used as part of the iRobot Beta Program unless you choose to participate. Any information collected under this program is subject to the same or functionally similar physical, administrative, and technological safeguards as other information collected under normal Robot use.

ANNEX 4 - THIRD-PARTY TRACKING AND ONLINE ADVERTISING

 

We collect personal information through certain tracking technologies. We set out below more information on cookies (a certain type of tracking technology) and our approach to third-party tracking and online advertising.

 

COOKIES

 

Cookies. Cookies are text files that are placed on your computer or other mobile or handheld device (such as smartphones or tablets) when you visit certain pages on the internet. They are used to “remember” when your computer or device accesses a website.

 

We use the following types of cookies:

 

    (i) Strictly necessary cookies. These are cookies that are required for the operation of our Website. They include, for example, cookies that enable you to log into areas of our Website and to add items into your shopping basket.

 

    (ii) Functionality cookies. These are used to recognize you when you return to our Website so that we can offer you a better shopping experience on this Website. For instance, this enables us to personalize our content for you, greet you by name and remember your preferences (for example, your choice of language or region).

 

    (iii) Analytical/performance cookies. These allow us to recognize and count the number of visitors and to see how visitors move around our Website when they are using it. This helps us to improve the way our Website works, for example, by ensuring that users are finding what they are looking for easily.

 

    (iv) Targeting cookies. These cookies enable us to collect information that we can use to make our Website and the advertising displayed on it, and the marketing messages we send to you more relevant to your interests. We may also share this information with third parties who provide a service to us for this purpose.

 

    (v) Third party cookies. Please be aware that advertisers and other third parties may use their own cookie tags when you click on an advertisement or link on our website. These third parties are responsible for setting out their own cookie and privacy policies.

 

Some jurisdictions require prior, opt-in consent before non-essential cookies may load. We comply with applicable regulations, and allow all website visitors to opt in or out of cookies and other trackers from within our cookie consent banner settings. Furthermore, most browsers will allow you to: (i) change your browser settings to notify you when you receive a cookie, which lets you choose whether to accept it; (ii) disable existing cookies; or (iii) set your browser to automatically reject cookies; however, doing so may negatively impact your experience using the Service, as some features and services on our Service may not work properly.
