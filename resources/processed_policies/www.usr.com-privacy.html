Terminology used

USR’s data protection and privacy measures are governed by the (i) the General Data Protection Regulation ((EU) 2016/679) ( “GDPR” ) and any national implementing laws, regulations and secondary legislation, as amended or updated from time to time, in the UK and then (ii) any successor legislation to the GDPR or the Data Protection Act 1998 ( “Data Protection Legislation" ).

For the purpose of Data Protection Legislation:

  1. where personal data is provided directly to USR through use of its Websites, email, or other means where USR is determining the way in which that personal data is processed, then USR will be a data controller of such information; and
  2. where USR is provided personal data in its capacity of providing Services to its customers, then USR will only process that personal data in accordance with the instructions of its customers and USR will therefore act as a data processor in respect of such personal data; USR’s customer will be the data controller of that personal data for that purpose.

Where USR acts as a data processor on behalf of its customers, USR will process that personal data on the instructions of the customer, who would have collected that personal data in accordance with that customer’s own privacy policy. The data subject should therefore check the customer’s own privacy policy, to ensure they understand how their personal data may be processed.

Your rights

Under Data Protection Legislation, you have the following rights in certain circumstances:

  • The right to be informed – this is information on USR’s collection and use of your personal data.
  • The right of access – you have the right to be provided with copies of the personal data of you that USR is processing as well as confirmation of the processing USR is doing. You can do this by sending a “subject access request” to the contact details noted above for USR’s consideration.
  • The right to rectification – if you think the personal data that USR holds on you is wrong, you can inform USR and USR will fix it.
  • The right to erasure (also known as the right to be forgotten) – if you want USR to permanently delete the personal data it holds about you then you can ask USR to do so.
  • The right to restrict processing – if you do not like how USR is using your personal data then you can let USR know and USR will stop processing it in that way.
  • The right to data portability – if you want USR to pass on your personal data to someone else then please let USR know. This transfer should not affect the integrity or otherwise damage your personal data.
  • The right to withdraw your consent – you can withdraw your consent for USR to process your personal data (if USR has relied on your consent to process your personal data) at any time by contacting USR. If USR has relied only on your consent as the basis to process your personal data then USR will stop processing your personal data at the point you withdraw your consent. Please note that if USR can also rely on other bases to process your personal data aside from consent then USR may do so even if you have withdrawn your consent.
  • Rights in relation to automated decision making and profiling – if USR use either automated decision making or profiling then you have a right to know. Also, USR needs your consent if either of these are used to make a decision that affects you. As with all consent, you can withdraw it at any time.

To exercise any of the above rights please complete the online form at the bottom of this page. (NOTE: this email is for GDPR and Privacy related requests. For product compliance inquiries contact usr_product_compliance@usr.com )

Where you exercise your right to erasure or where information is deleted in accordance with USR’s retention policy, please note that after the deletion of your personal data, it cannot be recovered, so if you require a copy of this personal data, please request this during the period USR retains the data.

Where you exercise your right to request access to the information USR processes about you, you will not have to pay a fee to access your personal data (or to exercise any of the other rights). However, USR may charge a reasonable fee if your request is clearly unfounded, repetitive or excessive.

Alternatively, USR may refuse to comply with your request in these circumstances.

USR will try to respond to all legitimate access requests within one month. Occasionally it may take longer than a month if your request is particularly complex or you have made a number of requests. In this case, USR will notify you and keep you updated.
